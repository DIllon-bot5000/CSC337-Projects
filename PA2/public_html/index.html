Epic Poems: Dante's Inferno
Dante's Inferno Paradise Lost

Dante's Inferno is an epic poem written by Dante Alighieri in the 14th century and is a part of a larger work called the Divine Comedy. Inferno follows the journey of the author as he travels through Hell following the poet Virgil. Dante travels through the nine circles of Hell learning what happens to perpetrators of various sins. Dante also takes time to interact with various historical figures as well as some of his fellow countrymen. In the interactions with his countrymen Dante discusses current events of his life as well as taking shots at some of his political enemies. Allegorically, the Inferno describes the recognition and rejection of sin on the path towards God.

The Circles of Hell
First Circle: Limbo Fourth Circle: Greed Seventh Circle: Violence
Second Circle: Lust Fifth Circle: Wrath Eighth Circle: Fraud
Third Circle: Gluttony Sixth Circle: Heresy Ninth Circle: Treachery
